I found the love of my life. She wasn’t what I expected

A love letter to an unexpected recipient

The first thing I noticed was her smile. Bright, shining. You could feel the joy radiating out of her. And then her big, brown eyes would stare you down. I noticed the twinkle in the corner. Her eyes would smile at you too

Her arms, warm in embrace. When she brought me near her. When she enveloped me in her hug, it’s like I was the only person in the world that mattered.

Her hair, a deep raven. Flowed with the waves of a thousand mountains. Yet at touch, was soft as cotton snow.

She began talking to me. And I started to see this beautifully imperfect creature.

Impassioned speeches about how she wanted to improve the lives of people around her.

How she wanted to stick it to the man. Yet still felt the lure of traditionality.

She talked about her dark moments. How she couldn’t see the light behind the clouds.

How she hated her body and would deprive it of the love it deserved.

That her heart had worked to pump love into others. But barely pumped for herself.

I could tell she was the last person she tried to make happy.

My heart ached a little for her.

But she didn’t want my pity.

She’d laugh it off and say it’s okay.

Darkness was only one chapter in her book of life.

She brought me into the chapter of witty remarks and the occasional dark humor.

She’s more twisted than I thought.

Then came her curiosity.

She asked me these probing questions.Who am I? What’s my story? Who do I want to be?

She wanted to know what I was beyond what I did.

And then she flew me over to her thoughts on Chinese philosophy and how it’s shaped her life views.

She stopped trying to find herself. She stopped trying to find passion.

“Then what do you do instead?”

“I just be.”

“I follow my curiosities.”

“I look for the moments of Karen.”

I felt the weight of her sigh as she closed her eyes.

She said there’s a lot of pressure in her world to succeed.

That her tip jar of potential has been overflowing but she doesn’t know how to cash out.

Stopping the pursuit of the next thing and the next thing was what saved her.

She learned how to enjoy her present again.

There’s that smile again.

She starting laughing at her own quirks.

How she couldn’t stand slow texters. She said she’s working on this.

How she’d go on four tangents in a story.

How she was a ditz in real life and always tripped on herself.

But liked to give off the air of cool, calm and composed.

Love came up.

Her heart had been broken a couple times.

Once because it was for the best. Once because she was lied to.

There she asked me, what I thought of love.

“I’m still figuring it out.”

“Me too.”

But in that moment, I knew I had fallen for her.

I had fallen for the joy, the passion, the ambition.

I had fallen for her impatience, her over communication, her really bad jokes.

I had fallen for the healing cracks, the jagged edges, the dark crevices.

I loved her.

And this her was me.

Thanks for reading! This is a friendly reminder to love yourself first :) I’ve been hard on myself lately so I decided to write a love letter to myself. It’s important to be our own lovers, and to remind ourselves we’re loved and lovable.
